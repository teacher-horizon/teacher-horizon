(~5-10 min)

The goal here is to start a conversation and have a sort of ice breaker, making the student comfortable with the ideas that will follow in the Vocabulary and Input.

Remember that all the questions in the Warm-Up section are meant for the student to bring personal ideas to the class, to be later related to the content just learned.

Also, take a look at the Vocabulary beforehand and make sure to show how important the content of this Lesson is in the real world. For example: we are constantly greeting people, therefore knowing how to greet a stranger in English is a must since day one.

(~10 min)

Present the new vocabulary to your student in a dynamic way. Make sure to say the words out loud and have your student repeat them.

Also, have a moment where only the student says the vocabulary and use it to correct possible mispronunciations.

(~20 min)

See “Grammar Documentation” in the Documents area of your dashboard for Lesson Plans prepared by the Horizon Group.

(~20 min)

This is the Conversation part of your class. Make sure to make your student speak as much English as possible, considering the content just seen. Help with structurally wrong sentences by repeating the same information in the correct structure. Make sure to ask if your student has any remaining questions regarding the Lesson.

| Comparatives

Short Adjectives add "er"

I am taller than you.

She is shorter than me.

I am happier now.

I am stronger than my brother.

Long Adjectives [more/less + (adj)]

You are more intelligent than me.

She is more beaultiful than her mother.

I am more patient than him.

We are more stressed than last year.

| There to Be

Affirmative

There is a song playing.

There is a car in the garage.

There are people in this house.

There are toys on the couch.

Negative

There is not a song playing.

There isn't a car in the garage.

There are not people in this house.

There aren't toys on the couch.

Interrogative

Is there a song playing?

Is there a car in the garage?

Are there people in this house?

Are there toys on the couch?

| Superlatives

Short Adjectives add "est"

I am the tallest in my family.

She is the shortest in the group.

I am the happiest person I know.

He is the strongest superhero.

Long Adjectives [most/least + (adj)]

You are the most intelligent person here.

She is the most beaultiful actress.

I am the most patient teacher.

We are the most stressed students ever.
